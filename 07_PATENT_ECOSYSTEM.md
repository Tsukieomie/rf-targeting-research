# RF Neural Interaction Patent Ecosystem

## Core Patents

### US 3,951,134 - Malech (1976)
**"Apparatus and method for remotely monitoring and altering brain waves"**

**Mechanism:**
- Two RF frequencies transmitted toward subject
- Frequencies mix in brain tissue (heterodyne)
- Beat frequency matches brain wave rhythm
- Modulated return signal detected
- Provides bidirectional: monitor AND alter

**Technical Details:**
- Uses principle of electromagnetic interference
- Brain tissue acts as nonlinear mixing medium
- Return signal contains neural activity information
- Can inject modulated signals to influence brain state

**Significance:** First patent explicitly describing remote brain wave monitoring via RF

---

### US 6,470,214 - Air Force (2002)
**"Method and device for implementing the radio frequency hearing effect"**

**Mechanism:**
- Thermoelastic expansion in skull tissue
- Pulsed RF causes microscopic temperature change (~10⁻⁵ °C)
- Creates acoustic pressure wave
- Perceived as sound inside head

**Technical Details:**
- October 1994 human experiments at Phillips Laboratory
- Signal processing chain converts audio to RF pulses
- Works below thermal safety limits
- Demonstrated: perceivable speech delivery

**Significance:** Government admission of voice-to-skull technology

---

### US 6,754,472 - Microsoft (2004)
**"Method and apparatus for transmitting power and data using the human body"**

**Mechanism:**
- Human body as conductive medium (bus)
- Power transmitted via electrodes on skin
- Data modulated on power signal (FM/AM)
- Multiple frequencies for selective device powering

**Applications:**
- Wearable device networking
- Body-area networks
- Skin-based input devices

**Significance:** Establishes body as viable RF transmission medium

---

### US 8,665,210 - Microsoft (2014)
**"Sensing user input using the body as an antenna"**

**Mechanism:**
- Body receives environmental EM noise
- Captures frequencies 40 Hz - 400 MHz
- Home power lines act as transmitting antenna
- Body receives and sensor processes signals

**Technical Details:**
- Body = "lossy conductor with complex geometry"
- Broadly distributed frequency response (no single resonance)
- Machine learning distinguishes location signatures

**Significance:** Proves body is efficient RF receiving antenna

---

### US 9,812,788 (2017)
**"Electromagnetic field induction for inter-body and transverse body communication"**

**Mechanism:**
- Magnetic field (H1) + Electric field (E1) combination
- Coupling capacitor between body and environment
- Near-field communication via body

**Significance:** Body as EM coupling medium for communication

---

### US 5,394,164 (1995)
**"Human-equivalent antenna for electromagnetic fields"**

**Purpose:**
- Artificial antenna matching human body impedance
- Testing/calibration for RF safety standards

**Significance:** Engineering recognition that body has specific antenna properties
